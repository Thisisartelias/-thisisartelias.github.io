My Story

I started making art when I was ten years old. I would draw lines on paper. This was something I did for fun.. It did not take long for me to think more about what I was doing.

By the time I was eleven years old I had a plan for my art. I wanted to sell my drawings. So I started selling them in the city center. This was a deal for me. It helped me learn about being creative. It also helped me want to do more.

When I was twelve years old I had my first art exhibition. Seeing my art on the wall was really cool. My art has also been featured in a local magazine (around age 12). It made me want to do even better. So I started making art that was bigger and more detailed.

Now my art is shown in different places. You can see it in community spaces. Even in a barber shop and a social work organization building.

Today I make abstract paintings, for people who like art that is one of a kind. I want each painting to be unique and to stand out. Abstract art is what I love to do. It started with random lines but now it is a big part of my life.
